PART B: Case-Based Questions (2)

Case-Based Question 1: The author was pushed into the swimming pool by a boy. He sank, struggled, panicked, and nearly drowned. The experience left a lasting fear in his mind.

How did this incident affect the author's life?

Answer: The incident created a deep-rooted fear of water in the author's mind. Even years later, the fear haunted him and affected his confidence. He avoided swimming and water bodies until he consciously decided to overcome this fear through training and determination.

Case-Based Question 2: The author hired an instructor who trained him step by step, helping him gain confidence in water.

What role did the instructor play in overcoming fear?

Answer: The instructor played a crucial role by using systematic and patient training. He taught proper breathing, strokes, and gradually removed fear. This structured guidance helped the author regain confidence and conquer his long-standing fear.

PART D: Short Answer Questions (5)

Why did the author fear water?

Answer: The author feared water due to a traumatic childhood incident at the YMCA swimming pool. A bully pushed him into deep water, causing panic and near drowning. This experience left a permanent psychological fear.

How did the author try to overcome his fear?

Answer: The author hired a professional swimming instructor who trained him step by step. Through regular practice, breathing exercises, and confidence building, he gradually overcame his fear of water.

What emotions did the author feel during the incident?

Answer: The author felt sudden panic, terror, and helplessness. His legs became stiff, and his lungs ached. He lost control and thought he would drown, which deeply scarred his mind.

What made the author confident later?

Answer: Consistent training, proper guidance, and determination helped the author gain confidence. Once he mastered swimming, he tested himself in lakes and pools to ensure the fear was gone.

What lesson does Deep Water teach?

Answer: The chapter teaches that fear can be conquered through courage, determination, and effort. Facing fears helps individuals live freely and confidently.

PART E: Long Answer Questions (3)

Describe the YMCA swimming pool incident.

Answer: The YMCA swimming pool incident was a turning point in the author's life. At the age of nine, he was pushed into the deep end by a bully. He sank, panicked, and struggled to breathe. His legs became paralyzed, and fear overwhelmed him. Though he survived, the incident left a deep psychological scar. The fear of water haunted him for years, restricting his freedom. This experience highlights how childhood trauma can deeply affect one's mind and behaviour.

How did the author finally overcome his fear of water?

Answer: The author overcame his fear by hiring a swimming instructor who trained him patiently. He learned breathing techniques, floating, and swimming strokes. Regular practice slowly removed fear from his mind. He challenged himself by swimming across lakes and pools to ensure confidence. His determination and structured learning helped him conquer fear completely.

Explain the theme of Deep Water.

Answer: Deep Water explores the theme of fear and its impact on human life. It shows how fear can paralyse a person mentally and physically. The chapter also emphasizes that fear can be overcome through determination, courage, and effort. The author's journey from terror to confidence inspires readers to face and defeat their fears.
