Case-Based Questions 2 Questions

Case-Based Question 1: Dr. Sadao finds a wounded enemy soldier on the beach. Why does Sadao decide to save him despite the risk?
Answer: Sadao saves the soldier because his duty as a doctor comes before patriotism. Humanity and medical ethics compel him to help a dying person regardless of nationality.
Case-Based Question 2: The General promises to send assassins but fails to do so. What does this reveal about the General's character?
Answer: The General is selfish and unreliable. He prioritizes his own comfort over duty, exposing hypocrisy within authority figures during war.

Short Answer Questions 5 Questions

1. Why was Sadao in danger for helping the soldier?
Answer: Helping an enemy soldier was considered treason during wartime. Sadao risked arrest, loss of reputation, and even death for choosing humanity over national loyalty.
2. How did Hana contribute to saving the soldier?
Answer: Hana overcame fear and disgust to nurse the wounded soldier. She helped clean wounds and assisted Sadao, showing compassion and courage.
3. Why did the servants leave the house?
Answer: The servants believed helping an enemy was wrong and dishonorable. Their loyalty to the nation overpowered their loyalty to Sadao.
4. What inner conflict does Sadao face?
Answer: Sadao struggles between his role as a loyal Japanese citizen and his ethical duty as a doctor. This conflict forms the core of the story.
5. How does the story criticize war?
Answer: The story shows war creates hatred and moral confusion. It highlights how humanity suffers when national pride overrides compassion.

Long Answer Questions 3 Questions

1. Discuss the theme of humanity in The Enemy.
Answer: The story emphasizes that humanity transcends national boundaries. Dr. Sadao risks his life to save an enemy soldier, proving that compassion is universal. Despite social pressure and legal danger, he follows his conscience. The narrative suggests that war dehumanizes people, but moral courage restores dignity and hope.
2. Analyze Dr. Sadao's character.
Answer: Dr. Sadao is intelligent, ethical, and courageous. As a doctor, he values life above politics. Though loyal to his country, he cannot abandon a dying man. His actions reveal strength of character and deep humanity.
3. How does the story present the conflict between duty and conscience?
Answer: The story portrays a powerful struggle between national duty and moral conscience. While the law demands loyalty to the nation, Sadao's conscience urges him to save a life. His choice highlights the supremacy of human values over blind patriotism.
