Case-Based Questions

Case 1: The poet notices her mother's pale and lifeless face while travelling.
What does this image convey about the poet's feelings?
The image reflects the poet's fear and anxiety about her mother's old age and approaching death. It shows her deep emotional attachment and the pain of realizing that separation is inevitable.
Case 2: The poet looks at energetic children and moving trees to distract herself.
Why does the poet shift her attention outside?
The poet shifts her attention to avoid confronting the painful reality of her mother's aging. The lively images contrast with her mother's weakness and provide temporary emotional relief.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why does the poet feel pain on seeing her mother?
The poet feels pain because her mother looks weak, pale, and aged. This reminds her of the inevitability of death. The realization of possible separation fills her with fear and sadness.
2. How does the poet try to overcome her fear?
The poet tries to distract herself by looking outside the car at the lively scenes of nature and children. These images provide a contrast to her mother's condition and help her temporarily escape painful thoughts.
3. Explain the significance of the 'winter moon'.
The 'winter moon' symbolizes old age, coldness, and lifelessness. By comparing her mother's face to a winter moon, the poet highlights her mother's aging and fading vitality.
4. Why does the poet smile and say "see you soon"?
The poet smiles to hide her inner pain and fear. Her words express hope and reassurance, though she is aware of the uncertainty of life and the possibility of separation.
5. What is the central idea of the poem?
The poem explores the poet's emotional response to her mother's aging. It highlights love, fear of loss, and the painful realization that death is inevitable.

Long Answer Questions

1. Discuss the theme of aging and separation in My Mother at Sixty-Six.
The poem highlights the poet's fear of losing her mother due to old age. Seeing her mother's pale face triggers anxiety about death and separation. The poet contrasts images of youth and vitality with her mother's weakness. Despite her fear, she hides her emotions with a smile. The poem reflects universal human experience—love for parents and the pain of watching them age.
2. How does Kamala Das use imagery to convey emotions?
Kamala Das uses powerful imagery such as the 'winter moon' to symbolize old age and lifelessness. The lively images of trees and children represent youth and energy. These contrasting images highlight the poet's inner conflict between hope and fear, making the emotions more intense and relatable.
3. Explain the significance of the ending of the poem.
The ending shows the poet smiling and saying "see you soon." This reflects emotional strength and acceptance. Although she fears separation, she chooses hope and reassurance over despair. The smile symbolizes her effort to stay strong for her mother.
