Case-Based Questions

Case 1: Franz notices villagers sitting quietly on the back benches during the class. They look sad and serious.

a) Why were villagers present in the classroom?

They came to attend the last French lesson to show respect for their language and teacher M. Hamel.

b) What did their presence symbolize?

It symbolized regret for neglecting their language and respect for M. Hamel and French culture.

Case 2: M. Hamel writes "Vive La France" on the blackboard and dismisses the class silently.

a) Why was he emotional at that moment?

It was his last lesson after 40 years of teaching French, as German would replace it from the next day.

b) What does this act show?

It shows deep patriotism and love for the French language, and serves as a final act of defiance against the Prussian order.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why was Franz scared while going to school?

Franz was scared because he had not prepared his lesson on participles. He expected his teacher to scold or punish him. Moreover, he was already late for school, which added to his fear.

2. Why was the classroom different that day?

The classroom was unusually quiet. Village elders were sitting on the back benches. M. Hamel wore special clothes. All this happened because it was the last French lesson before German became compulsory.

3. What order did the Prussian government pass?

The Prussian government ordered that only German would be taught in schools of Alsace and Lorraine. French language teaching was stopped, which deeply hurt the people and teachers like M. Hamel.

4. How did Franz's feelings about French change?

Initially Franz disliked French. But during the last lesson, he realized its beauty and importance. Losing the language made him regret not studying seriously earlier.

5. Why did M. Hamel blame himself and parents?

M. Hamel blamed himself for giving holidays and parents for sending children to work instead of school. He felt everyone had neglected education and language when there was time.

Long Answer Questions

1. Describe the role of M. Hamel as a teacher in the story.

M. Hamel represents a devoted and patriotic teacher. He served the school for forty years with dedication. On his last day, instead of showing anger, he taught with patience and dignity. He made students realize the importance of their mother tongue and blamed himself and parents for neglecting education. His emotional farewell and writing "Vive La France" showed his deep love for French language and country. M. Hamel believed that language is the key to identity and freedom. Through his actions, the author shows how teachers shape cultural pride and national spirit.

2. How does The Last Lesson highlight the importance of language?

The story highlights language as a symbol of identity, culture, and freedom. When French was banned, people realized its value. M. Hamel explained that language unites people and keeps them spiritually free even under foreign rule. Franz's regret shows how easily people ignore their heritage until it is taken away. The villagers' presence in class reflects collective guilt. The author emphasizes that preserving one's language is essential to preserve one's culture and national pride.

3. Explain Franz's transformation in the story.

Franz starts as a careless student who dislikes school and avoids learning. He fears punishment for not preparing his lesson. However, when he learns that it is the last French lesson, his attitude changes completely. He becomes attentive and emotional. He realizes the beauty of the French language and regrets wasting time. By the end, Franz understands the importance of education, language, and patriotism. His transformation shows how loss creates awareness and maturity.
