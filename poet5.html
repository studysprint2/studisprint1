Case-Based Questions

Case 1: Aunt Jennifer's fingers tremble while she embroiders the tigers.
What do her trembling fingers reveal about her life?
Her trembling fingers reveal fear, mental pressure, and oppression in her married life. They symbolize the burden of a patriarchal society that has crushed her confidence and freedom.
Case 2: The tigers continue to move proudly even after Aunt Jennifer's death.
What is the significance of this contrast?
The contrast shows that while Aunt Jennifer's life remains suppressed, her art expresses her hidden desire for freedom. The tigers symbolize eternal strength and courage.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why do Aunt Jennifer's fingers tremble?
Aunt Jennifer's fingers tremble due to fear and mental stress caused by her oppressive marriage. The burden of patriarchal control has weakened her confidence and freedom.
2. How do the tigers differ from Aunt Jennifer?
The tigers are bold, confident, and fearless, while Aunt Jennifer is weak and frightened. This contrast highlights her suppressed life and her desire for strength and independence.
3. What does the wedding band symbolize?
The wedding band symbolizes male dominance and the burden of marriage. It represents the oppressive control that restricts Aunt Jennifer's individuality and freedom.
4. How does embroidery become an escape for Aunt Jennifer?
Embroidery allows Aunt Jennifer to express her suppressed desires. Through her art, she creates powerful tigers that symbolize freedom and strength she lacks in real life.
5. What message does the poem convey?
The poem highlights the suffering of women in patriarchal marriages. It emphasizes the need for gender equality and freedom from oppression.

Long Answer Questions

1. Discuss the theme of oppression in Aunt Jennifer's Tigers.
The poem explores the oppression faced by women in a patriarchal society. Aunt Jennifer lives a fearful life dominated by her husband. Her trembling fingers and heavy wedding band symbolize her suppressed existence. However, her embroidered tigers represent strength and freedom. The contrast highlights how women's creativity becomes a medium of silent protest against injustice.
2. Explain the symbolism used in the poem.
Adrienne Rich uses powerful symbols such as tigers, wedding band, and embroidery. Tigers symbolize freedom and fearlessness, while the wedding band represents patriarchal oppression. Embroidery reflects artistic escape. These symbols effectively convey the poet's feminist message.
3. How does the poem present a feminist perspective?
The poem criticizes male dominance and female suppression in marriage. Aunt Jennifer's suffering represents countless women trapped in oppressive relationships. Through symbolism and contrast, the poet demands equality, independence, and empowerment for women.
