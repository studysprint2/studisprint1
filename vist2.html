Case-Based Questions

Case 1: The astrologer predicted that the king would die by a tiger.
How did the king react to this prediction?
The king became obsessed with killing tigers to disprove the prophecy. His arrogance and belief in power led him to challenge destiny instead of understanding its deeper meaning.
Case 2: The king was injured by a wooden tiger while playing with his son.
What irony does this situation highlight?
The irony is that after killing 99 real tigers, the king dies because of a wooden tiger. This shows the inevitability of fate and mocks human pride.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why did the king start killing tigers?
The king killed tigers to challenge the astrologer's prophecy. His pride and belief in power made him think he could control fate by eliminating the danger.
2. How does the story satirize autocratic rulers?
The story mocks rulers who misuse power for personal obsession. The king's foolish decisions and cruelty highlight the dangers of unchecked authority.
3. What role does the Diwan play?
The Diwan supports the king blindly and helps him continue tiger hunting. He represents loyal officials who enable a ruler's irrational behavior.
4. How is irony used in the story?
Irony is central as the king, who feared death by a tiger, is killed by a wooden one. This underlines the unpredictability of fate.
5. What message does the author convey?
The author warns against arrogance and misuse of power. Destiny cannot be avoided, and pride often leads to downfall.

Long Answer Questions

1. Discuss the theme of fate versus free will in The Tiger King.
The story highlights the conflict between fate and human pride. The king believes he can alter destiny by killing tigers. However, his efforts only confirm the prophecy. The wooden tiger ultimately causes his death, proving that fate is unavoidable. Kalki suggests that arrogance blinds humans and leads them to self-destruction.
2. Explain how satire is used to criticize authority.
Kalki uses humor and exaggeration to expose the foolishness of absolute power. The king's obsession, senseless killings, and blind advisors reflect how authority can become dangerous when unchecked. The satirical tone makes the criticism effective and memorable.
3. Describe the significance of the ending.
The ending reinforces the central message that destiny cannot be defeated. The king's death by a wooden tiger is ironic and symbolic. It shows that arrogance, cruelty, and misuse of power ultimately lead to downfall.
