Case-Based Questions

Case 1: Antarctica remains untouched and preserved compared to other continents.
Why is Antarctica considered crucial for understanding climate change?
Antarctica preserves Earth's ancient history in its ice layers. Studying it helps scientists understand climate patterns and predict future environmental changes caused by global warming.
Case 2: The author calls Antarctica a mirror to the world.
Explain the meaning of this statement.
Antarctica reflects the consequences of human actions. Damage to this pristine land warns humanity about the impact of environmental neglect on the entire planet.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why was the Students on Ice programme started?
The programme was started to educate young people about climate change. It aims to create environmental awareness by allowing students to experience Antarctica firsthand.
2. How does Antarctica help understand Earth's past?
Antarctica's ice layers store information about Earth's climate history. Scientists study them to understand how environmental changes occurred over millions of years.
3. What dangers does Antarctica face today?
Antarctica faces threats from global warming, melting ice, and increasing human activities. These changes can disturb the fragile ecosystem.
4. Why does the author call Antarctica a place of extremes?
Antarctica has extreme cold, strong winds, and long periods of darkness and light. These conditions make it one of the harshest environments on Earth.
5. What message does the chapter convey?
The chapter emphasizes environmental responsibility. It warns humans to protect nature before irreversible damage occurs.

Long Answer Questions

1. Describe the significance of Antarctica in understanding climate change.
Antarctica plays a vital role in understanding climate change because it preserves Earth's climatic history in its ice sheets. These ice layers provide evidence of temperature changes over millions of years. The continent acts as an early warning system, showing how human activities affect the environment. The author urges people to learn from Antarctica and adopt sustainable practices to protect the planet.
2. How does the author combine travel writing with environmental concern?
Tishani Doshi blends personal travel experiences with scientific facts. She describes the beauty of Antarctica while highlighting environmental threats. This combination makes the narrative engaging and informative, encouraging readers to care for nature.
3. What lessons does the journey to Antarctica offer humanity?
The journey teaches that Earth is fragile and interconnected. Damage to remote regions like Antarctica affects the entire planet. The chapter urges humans to act responsibly and preserve the environment for future generations.
