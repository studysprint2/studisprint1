Case-Based Question 1: Rajkumar Shukla insisted that Gandhi visit Champaran to see the plight of indigo farmers.

What does Rajkumar Shukla's role reveal about the peasants?

Answer: Rajkumar Shukla represents the determination and suffering of the peasants. Though poor and uneducated, he showed courage by approaching Gandhi. His persistence proves that awareness and courage can initiate social change.

Case-Based Question 2: Gandhi was ordered to leave Champaran but refused to obey.

Why was Gandhi's refusal significant?

Answer: Gandhi's refusal marked the beginning of civil disobedience in India. He showed that unjust laws should be challenged peacefully. This act empowered peasants and demonstrated moral courage against colonial authority.

PART D: Short Answer Questions (5)

Why did Gandhi visit Champaran?

Answer: Gandhi visited Champaran at the request of Rajkumar Shukla to investigate the problems of indigo farmers. British planters forced peasants to grow indigo under the exploitative Tinkathia system, causing immense hardship.

What was the Tinkathia system?

Answer: The Tinkathia system forced peasants to grow indigo on three-twentieths of their land and sell it at low prices to British planters. This system led to poverty, exploitation, and suffering among farmers.

How did Gandhi react to the official order?

Answer: Gandhi calmly refused to leave Champaran despite official orders. He accepted arrest but did not obey unjust authority, showing moral courage and commitment to justice.

Why was the refund of 25% important?

Answer: The 25% refund was significant not for money but as a moral victory. It showed that the British planters were wrong and peasants had the right to justice.

What role did lawyers play?

Answer: Lawyers supported Gandhi and initially charged high fees. Later, inspired by Gandhi, they decided to serve peasants selflessly and focus on social justice instead of profit.

PART E: Long Answer Questions (3)

Describe Gandhi's role in the Champaran movement.

Answer: Gandhi played a crucial role in the Champaran movement by leading a peaceful struggle against the exploitation of indigo farmers. He listened to peasants, defied unjust orders, and used non-violence as a tool of resistance. By forming an inquiry committee, he exposed the cruelty of the Tinkathia system. His calm leadership empowered peasants and made them fearless. The movement marked Gandhi's first successful civil disobedience in India and laid the foundation for future freedom struggles.

How did the Champaran episode become a landmark in India's freedom struggle?

Answer: The Champaran episode was significant because it showed the effectiveness of non-violent resistance. Gandhi proved that ordinary peasants could challenge colonial power through truth and courage. It awakened political consciousness among farmers and demonstrated that freedom begins with self-confidence and fearlessness. This movement inspired future mass struggles against British rule.

Explain the main theme of Indigo.

Answer: Indigo highlights the injustice faced by Indian peasants under British rule and the power of peaceful resistance. It shows how exploitation, fear, and poverty trapped farmers. Gandhi's intervention brought awareness, courage, and justice. The chapter emphasizes moral strength, leadership, and the idea that true freedom lies in overcoming fear and injustice.
