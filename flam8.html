Case-Based Questions

Case 1: Sophie often imagines a glamorous future far removed from her present life.
What does this reveal about Sophie's character?
This reveals Sophie's imaginative and dreamy nature. She uses fantasy to escape her harsh reality. Her dreams show ambition but also a lack of practical understanding of her social and economic limitations.
Case 2: Sophie waits by the canal hoping to meet Danny Casey.
Why is this scene important?
This scene highlights the contrast between fantasy and reality. Sophie's disappointment symbolizes the collapse of her dreams and her inability to accept the truth, marking a moment of emotional realization.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why did Sophie dream of owning a boutique?
Sophie dreamt of owning a boutique to escape her poor working-class life. She desired glamour, success, and social recognition. Her dreams reflected her dissatisfaction with her present condition and her wish for a better future.
2. Why was Jansie more realistic than Sophie?
Jansie was practical and aware of her social limits. She understood that working-class girls had limited opportunities. Unlike Sophie, she accepted reality and discouraged unrealistic dreams.
3. How did Sophie's family background affect her dreams?
Sophie came from a working-class family with limited resources. This made her dreams unrealistic. Her desire to escape poverty pushed her into fantasy rather than motivating her to plan realistically.
4. Describe Sophie's relationship with Geoff.
Sophie admired Geoff because he was quiet and mysterious. She believed he would understand her dreams. Geoff's silence made Sophie imagine him as a bridge to a more glamorous world.
5. What is the significance of Danny Casey in the story?
Danny Casey symbolizes Sophie's fantasies and aspirations. He represents success, glamour, and escape from reality. Sophie's imaginary meeting with him highlights her emotional vulnerability.

Long Answer Questions

1. Describe Sophie's character in Going Places.
Sophie is a young working-class girl full of dreams and imagination. She is ambitious but unrealistic, often escaping reality through fantasies. Her inability to accept her social limitations leads her to create imaginary stories. Though imaginative, she lacks practicality. Sophie represents youth trapped between ambition and reality.
2. Explain the theme of fantasy versus reality in the story.
The story highlights the contrast between Sophie's imagined world and her real life. Her fantasies offer temporary escape but ultimately lead to disappointment. Reality intrudes when her dreams collapse, emphasizing the dangers of living in illusion without practical effort.
3. What message does Going Places convey?
The story conveys that dreams without realistic planning can lead to disillusionment. While ambition is important, one must recognize social realities and work hard toward achievable goals. Escaping into fantasy cannot replace real effort.
