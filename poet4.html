Case-Based Questions

Case 1: The roadside stand is ignored by passing motorists who complain about its appearance.
What does this reveal about city people's attitude?
It reveals the indifference and selfishness of city people. They focus on comfort and cleanliness while ignoring the poverty and struggles of rural people who depend on the stand for survival.
Case 2: The poet criticizes 'greedy good-doers' who claim to help villagers.
Why does the poet oppose such people?
The poet opposes them because their help is selfish and harmful. Instead of improving villagers' lives, they exploit them and force unwanted changes, increasing rural suffering.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why was the roadside stand set up?
The roadside stand was set up by poor villagers to earn money. They hoped passing motorists would buy their goods and help improve their financial condition.
2. How do motorists react to the roadside stand?
Most motorists ignore the stand. Some stop only to ask for directions or complain about dirt, showing lack of empathy for villagers' hardships.
3. What is the poet's attitude toward rural people?
The poet shows deep sympathy toward rural people. He understands their pain and criticizes social inequality that keeps them poor and ignored.
4. Explain the phrase 'greedy good-doers'.
The phrase refers to people who pretend to help the poor but actually exploit them. Their actions worsen rural conditions instead of improving them.
5. What message does the poem convey?
The poem highlights social injustice and the gap between urban and rural life. It urges genuine compassion and fair economic opportunities for rural people.

Long Answer Questions

1. Discuss the theme of social inequality in A Roadside Stand.
The poem exposes the economic gap between urban and rural people. Rural villagers struggle for basic needs while city people enjoy comfort and wealth. The roadside stand symbolizes rural hope and desperation. The poet criticizes the indifference of urban society and false helpers. The poem urges genuine concern for rural development.
2. How does Robert Frost portray rural suffering?
Frost portrays rural suffering through simple yet powerful images of poor villagers waiting for customers. Their hopes are crushed by careless motorists. The poet's sympathetic tone emphasizes emotional and economic pain caused by social inequality.
3. Explain the significance of the roadside stand.
The roadside stand represents the villagers' hope for survival and dignity. It highlights their desire for fair opportunities. Ignored by city people, it becomes a symbol of rural neglect and economic injustice.
